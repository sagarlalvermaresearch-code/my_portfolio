About Me

Kathmandu, Nepal

I am a research scholar specializing in immunology and viral pathology, currently seeking PhD opportunities where I can contribute to understanding how the immune system responds—and sometimes fails—in the face of viral infection.

02 — Research

Research Work

Systematic, rigorous investigation of immunological mechanisms in viral disease — from COVID-19 pathogenesis to ongoing Long COVID inquiry.

Ongoing · 2022–Present Primary Research

Immune Dysregulation in Post-Viral Syndromes (Long COVID) — Systematic Review

A comprehensive PRISMA 2020-guided systematic review investigating the immunological mechanisms underlying Long COVID and post-acute sequelae of SARS-CoV-2 infection. This work examines persistent immune activation, cytokine dysregulation, autoimmune phenomena, and the role of viral reservoirs in driving chronic symptoms. AI-assisted tools including Elicit and Rayyan are employed for rigorous literature screening across PubMed, Google Scholar, and related databases.

PRISMA 2020 Long COVID Cytokine Dysregulation Post-viral Syndromes Immune Activation AI-assisted Screening
2020–2021 · M.Sc. Dissertation Completed

COVID-19: A Systematic Review of Pathogenesis, Immunity & Therapeutics

Master's dissertation conducted under supervision of Dr. Shankar Mondal (AIIMS, New Delhi). Synthesized immunological, virological, and clinical literature to understand SARS-CoV-2 infection dynamics, host immune responses, and therapeutic strategies during the height of the global pandemic.

COVID-19 SARS-CoV-2 Immunopathology Systematic Review
Ongoing · Independent Analysis

Host–Pathogen Interaction Mapping in Emerging Viral Diseases

Ongoing analysis of mechanisms by which emerging viruses hijack, evade, and modulate host immune responses. Focus on identifying conserved immune evasion strategies and potential therapeutic targets across viral families.
